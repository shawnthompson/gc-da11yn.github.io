Administration

Project managers and administrators who ensure accessibility is integrated into processes, governance, and organizational culture for successful accessibility outcomes.

Related roles:

  • Product Ownership
  • Project Management
  • Governance

Administration and Accessibility

The Administration Role Group covers managing the product and project, as well as other organizational functions that often have a larger mandate than any individual project. Most administrative roles have very little direct involvement with the design, implementation, or testing of accessibility principles, but are instrumental in ensuring that team members actively making content accessible can be successful.

Product Ownership

Product owners own individual products and define their features. They are key to defining the importance of accessibility on a project and ensuring that products meet both business and user needs, including accessibility requirements.

Product Owner Responsibilities:

  • Prioritizing accessibility features in product backlogs
  • Defining accessibility acceptance criteria for user stories
  • Understanding cost and resource implications of accessibility requirements
  • Ensuring accessibility is included in product roadmaps and planning
  • Balancing accessibility needs with other business requirements
  • Communicating accessibility value to stakeholders

Project Management

Project managers are responsible for keeping the process of building accessible products organized and on track. They ensure accessibility is built into estimates, user stories, and requirements documents.

Project Manager Responsibilities:

  • Including accessibility tasks in project planning and timelines
  • Ensuring accessibility is considered in resource allocation and budgeting
  • Facilitating communication between accessibility specialists and team members
  • Tracking accessibility-related deliverables and milestones
  • Managing accessibility testing and review cycles
  • Coordinating accessibility training for team members
  • Risk management related to accessibility compliance

Governance

Unlike other administrative roles, governance is not part of individual project teams. Their mandate focuses on enterprise-wide initiatives and organizational accessibility policies and standards.

Governance Responsibilities:

  • Developing organizational accessibility policies and standards
  • Ensuring legal compliance with accessibility regulations
  • Establishing accessibility procurement requirements
  • Creating accessibility training and awareness programs
  • Monitoring organizational accessibility conformance
  • Managing accessibility-related legal and compliance issues
  • Setting accessibility goals and metrics for the organization

Administrative Focus Areas

  • Planning: Integrating accessibility into project and product planning
  • Resource Management: Ensuring adequate resources for accessibility work
  • Communication: Facilitating accessibility discussions and decisions
  • Training: Ensuring team members have accessibility knowledge
  • Compliance: Meeting legal and organizational accessibility requirements
  • Culture: Building organizational commitment to accessibility

Key Deliverables

Product Ownership:

  • Product roadmaps with accessibility milestones
  • User stories with accessibility acceptance criteria
  • Feature prioritization including accessibility requirements
  • Product requirements with accessibility specifications

Project Management:

  • Project plans with accessibility tasks and timelines
  • Resource allocation plans including accessibility expertise
  • Risk management plans addressing accessibility challenges
  • Progress reports including accessibility metrics
  • Training plans for accessibility skill development

Governance:

  • Organizational accessibility policies and procedures
  • Accessibility compliance frameworks and standards
  • Procurement guidelines with accessibility requirements
  • Training programs and accessibility awareness materials
  • Accessibility audit and monitoring reports
  • Legal compliance documentation and risk assessments

Success Factors

  • Clear accessibility goals and expectations
  • Adequate budget and timeline allocation for accessibility work
  • Regular communication about accessibility priorities
  • Access to accessibility expertise and training
  • Integration of accessibility into standard processes
  • Executive support for accessibility initiatives
  • Measurement and tracking of accessibility outcomes
